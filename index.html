Grace Hopper

A computing icon

Rear Admiral Grace Hopper, 1984 - Wikipedia

Here's a timeline of Grace Hopper's life:

  • 1906: Born in New York City, NY
  • 1928: graduated Phi Beta Kappa from Vassar in 1928 with a bachelor's degree in mathematics
  • 1930: Earned her master's degree at Yale University
  • 1934: Earned a Ph.D. in mathematics from Yale
  • 1930: Married New York University professor Vincent Foster Hopper
  • 1931: Began teaching mathematics at Vassar
  • 1941: Promoted to associate professor
  • 1943: Obtained a leave of absence from Vassar and was sworn into the United States Navy Reserve
  • 1944: Graduated first in her class in, and was assigned to the Bureau of Ships Computation Project at Harvard University as a lieutenant, junior grade.
  • 1945: Divorced her husband, Vincent Foster Hopper
  • 1949: Joined Eckert–Mauchly Computer Corporation
  • 1952: Published her first paper on the compilers
  • 1954: Named company's first director of automatic programming
  • 1967 to 1977: Served as the director of the Navy Programming Languages Group in the Navy's Office of Information Systems Planning
  • 1966: Retired from the Naval Reserve with the rank of commander at age 60
  • 1967: Recalled to active duty
  • 1971: Retires for a second time
  • 1972: Returns to active duty from second retirement
  • 1973: Promoted to the rank of captain
  • 1986: Admiral Hopper retired (involuntarily) from the Navy, after a career over 42 years
  • 1992: Dies at the age of 85

Vice Admiral Hopper left a legacy that paved a path for many women and men to follow. In 1969, she won the first “man of the year” award from the Data Processing Management Association. I appreciate the opportunity to pause for a few nanoseconds during Women’s History Month to acknowledge her contributions. -Linda Cureton, CIO, NASA
